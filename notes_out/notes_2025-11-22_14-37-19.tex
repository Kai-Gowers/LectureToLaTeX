Let $G$ be a group. Let $H \triangleleft G$. Let $K \triangleleft G$ with $K \subseteq H$.

Then:
\begin{align*}
N_G(K) &= \{ g \in G \mid gKg^{-1} = K \} \\
N_H(K) &= \{ h \in H \mid hKh^{-1} = K \}
\end{align*}

We have $N_H(K) = N_G(K) \cap H$.

Since $K \triangleleft G$, we have $N_G(K) = G$.

So $N_H(K) = G \cap H = H$.

Therefore $K \triangleleft H$.
